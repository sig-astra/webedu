Grammar

A gerund is a verb which functions as a noun by adding the “ing” ending. This can get a bit tricky because by itself, a gerund looks like a present-tense verb. But, what makes it a gerund is how it functions in the sentence. To help identify whether you have gerund or not, break the sentence down into its most basic form. Who is the subject, and what is the main verb/action being performed? Once you identify the main verb, it’s much easier to see a gerund because it isn’t what a subject is doing but rather complementing or having an action taken out on it.
